Your insurance

This tells us which protections apply to you, and lets us catch surprise bills and charges above what your plan says you owe.

If you were in-network but billed as out-of-network — or it was an emergency — you may be protected from a "surprise" bill by federal law.
An EOB (Explanation of Benefits) is the statement your insurance company sends you — not the hospital bill. It shows what they paid and what you actually owe. Adding it lets us catch surprise bills and charges above what your plan says you owe.
